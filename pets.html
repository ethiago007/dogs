History of Dogs

The dog is a domesticated descendant of wolves which is characterised by an upturning tail. The dog derived from an ancient,extinct, and the modern grey wolf is the dog's nearest relative. The dog was the first species to be domesticated, by hunters over 15,000 years ago, before the development of agriculture.

Due to theirnlong association with humans, dogs have expanded to a large number of domestic individuals. Dogs have over the years become uniquely adapted to human behaviours, hereby improving communication between the dog and man also strenghtening human-canine bond. Dog has been selectively bred over millennia for various behaviors, sensory capabilities, and physical attributes. Dog breeds vary widely in shape, size and color. They perform many roles for humans, such as hunting, herding, pulling loads, protection, assisting police and the military,companionship,therapy and aiding disabled people. This influence on human society has given them the nickname "man's best friend".

Dogs scientific classification Kingdom: Animalia Phylum: Chordata Class: Mammalia Order: Carnivora Family: Canidae Genus: canis Species: C.familiaris
